# Impeccable — Project Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Reviewers and product/engineering staff at Agentnoon / Dayforce evaluating a hiring assessment, plus the archetypal end user: a workforce/people-analytics PM or org-design lead exploring a 40,000-person company. Context of use: daytime, desktop, office. They read dense org charts and cost tables for a living, so they notice both data clarity and design craft. The interface must be impressive and original *from their perspective* — people who see generic org-chart SaaS constantly.

### Brand Personality
Exact, structural, quietly confident. The voice of a precise instrument or an architect's drawing — measured, unsentimental, never decorative for its own sake. It should evoke trust and craft, not flash. Stance: **neutral & original** (its own identity), with at most a subtle, tasteful nod to the company — never an approximate clone of their brand.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Org as Architecture" — drafting precision with editorial confidence.** Treat the organization like a precisely surveyed structure: architectural/blueprint sensibility, fine measured rules, confident editorial typography, intentional asymmetry. Explicitly NOT another rounded-card, evenly-padded dashboard. Anti-references: generic SaaS admin templates, neon-on-dark "AI" dashboards, uniform card grids, glassmorphism.

- **Theme:** Light, paper-tinted (never pure white/black). Fully token-driven (semantic OKLCH tokens, `light-dark()`-ready) so a dark mode is a later additive pass, not a rewrite. Dark is NOT a first-class priority now.
- **Type:** Display = **Archivo** (structural grotesque; big, confident; expanded cut for signature moments). UI/body = **Hanken Grotesk** (humanist, excellent tabular figures). Real hierarchy (≥1.25 scale steps). Tabular numerics for all aligned data. No monospace-as-decoration. Avoid the AI-reflex fonts (Inter, DM, Plus Jakarta, Fraunces, Playfair, Space/IBM Plex, Outfit, Instrument, etc.).
- **Color:** OKLCH. Paper-tinted light canvas, **ink-blue** primary, neutrals tinted toward the brand hue for cohesion. One sharp accent — warm **ochre/amber** reserved for cost emphasis (the 10% in 60-30-10). Red reserved strictly for over-threshold / destructive. No gray text on colored surfaces.
- **Signature moment:** the **organizational silhouette** — Giga Corp is dramatically bottom-heavy (~25k at level 9), so the *shape of the company* becomes a bold hero graphic in the analytics view. Org-chart connectors rendered as fine architectural drafting lines (not default beziers). One orchestrated load: staggered reveal of the executive layer.
- **Layout:** asymmetric editorial rhythm, varied spacing on a 4pt scale, grid broken intentionally for emphasis. Not everything in a card; no nested cards.

### Design Principles
1. **Structure is the subject.** Every visual choice should reinforce hierarchy, layers, and span — the thing the product is actually about.
2. **Data-first, then distinctive.** Legibility of 40k-row data is non-negotiable; personality comes from type, composition, and a few signature moments, never from obscuring data.
3. **Precision over decoration.** Fine rules, measured spacing, tabular numbers. If an element doesn't carry meaning, remove it. (No accent side-stripes, no gradient text, no decorative sparklines.)
4. **One memorable moment.** Spend the boldness budget on the organizational-silhouette hero and the drafting-line org chart — not scattered everywhere.
5. **Token-driven and accessible.** Semantic OKLCH tokens (dark-ready), WCAG AA contrast, visible focus, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, charts never rely on color alone.
